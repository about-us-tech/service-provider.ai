Temporary Branch Representative Agreement

This Temporary Branch Representative Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of [Insert Date], by and between:

Part A: ____________________________

Part B: ____________________________

1. Purpose

Part A engages Part B as a temporary representative to support general business communications, particularly in English-language contexts. This role is intended to be collaborative and support-focused, and does not initially carry decision-making authority unless explicitly granted by Part A in writing. Subject to Part A’s discretion and future needs, Part B may be considered for expanded responsibilities or delegated authority.

2. Scope of Responsibilities

2.1 Part B may assist with:

  • Routine communications with external contacts
  • Informal liaison and clarification tasks

2.2 Part B is not expected or authorized to:

  • Enter into contracts
  • Make financial or legal decisions
  • Act on behalf of Part A in official negotiations unless explicitly instructed in writing by Part A.

2.3 At Part A’s discretion, and depending on performance or need, Part B may be invited to:

  • Temporarily act as a nominee holder of shares (name only, no ownership interest)
  • Attend business meetings on behalf of Part A

In all such cases, Part B agrees to act in good faith and in alignment with Part A’s interests and intentions.

3. Assignment & Communication

Part A will notify Part B at least 1 day in advance when requesting specific support, including task details. No ongoing duties are required unless tasks are assigned.

4. Compensation

  • 4.1 Part B will be paid USD $15/hour based on actual time worked.
  • 4.2 Payment may be settled after each 8 hours accumulated, or as mutually agreed.
  • 4.3 Alternatively, specific tasks may be compensated at a flat rate between USD $10–$50, depending on complexity.
  • 4.4 No payment is owed unless a task is assigned and completed.

5. Liability and Good Faith Cooperation

  • 5.1 Part B will not be held responsible for business risks or outcomes relating to Part A’s operations.
  • 5.2 However, both parties agree to:
    • Act professionally and in good faith
    • Avoid any unauthorized or misleading actions
    • Clarify roles before taking steps beyond ordinary support

6. Confidentiality

  • 6.1 Part B agrees to keep all non-public information shared by Part A confidential.
  • 6.2 This includes business information, documents, communications, and the existence or nature of this Agreement.
  • 6.3 Part B should not disclose any such information to third parties without written permission from Part A.
  • 6.4 Reasonable discretion and confidentiality shall be maintained even after termination.

7. Flexibility and Duration

This agreement is non-exclusive and non-binding long-term. Part A reserves the right to adjust, end, or replace the temporary representative arrangement at any time, depending on business needs, with no hard commitment expected from either side.

8. Independent Status

This Agreement does not create an employment, partnership, or legal agency relationship. Part B is acting as an independent support resource.

9. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the jurisdiction where Part B is lawfully and permanently domiciled at the time of signing, unless otherwise mutually agreed.

10. Entire Understanding

This Agreement represents the complete understanding between the parties and may only be amended in writing by both sides.
